Executive Core Findings

Are customers generally happy with Mr. Biggs?
No, not with their current state. Customers are deeply in love with the historical memory of Mr. Biggs, but explicitly unhappy with its modern decline, general absence, and perceived collapse of consistency.

The Defining Emotion: Nostalgia
Nostalgia represents exactly 50% of all individual comment tones. The brand holds unparalleled emotional real estate across generations of Nigerian consumers. Customers associate the name with childhood wins, family gatherings, and high-quality pastry standards ("when meat pie was actual meat pie, not potato fill").

Strategic Actionable Recommendation

Execute a "Retro Revival" Inversion Strategy: The data proves that franchising broke the brand's quality controls. Mr. Biggs should immediately launch a highly visible, corporate-owned flagship store footprint in major hubs (like Abuja or Lagos) anchored around marketing the "Original Recipe Meat Pie". Do not use standard franchise partners for this launch. Pair this with aggressive digital content assets reminding consumers of the "good old days" to convert passive nostalgia into foot traffic.
