Overall customer sentiment

Mixed-positive nostalgia. Customers strongly admire the old Mr. Biggs experience, especially the meat pie, but they express concern and dissatisfaction about the brand’s decline, inactivity, inconsistent quality, and reduced presence.

Are customers generally happy? Not fully. They are happy with the memory and legacy of Mr. Biggs, but not generally happy with the current state of the brand.

One specific recommendation

Relaunch the original meat pie as a hero product. Restore and standardize the original recipe, pilot it in visible Lagos/Abuja locations, enforce freshness/service SOPs, then run a nostalgia-led social campaign with a brand ambassador and branch-availability updates.

Findings

Most discussed topics

  • Nostalgia/childhood memories: the biggest theme; many comments mention “old days,” childhood, ads, school memories, and emotional attachment.
  • Meat pie taste/quality: the dominant product conversation; customers describe it as big, sweet, unique, legendary, and unmatched.
  • Brand decline/inactivity: customers ask whether Mr. Biggs is still around, inactive, sold, closed, or no longer visible.
  • Quality consistency/franchising: a smaller but serious complaint links decline to franchise inconsistency, stale food, poor service, and facilities.

Complaints and compliments

  • Main compliments: original meat pie, unique taste, aroma, crunchiness, scotch egg, fried rice, spicy chicken, spaghetti, ice cream, childhood experience, and memorable ads.
  • Main complaints: hard/dry pastry, loss of original quality, inactive brand presence, poor freshness, deteriorating facilities, staff hospitality, hot drinks, stale/rotten food mentions, and lack of aggressive marketing.
  • Overall emotion: nostalgia best represents the audience because most positive and mixed comments are anchored in remembered childhood experiences, former product quality, and longing for a comeback.
