The verdict

Customers love the memory of Mr Biggs — and are frustrated it's gone

Overall sentiment leans positive (33 of 57 comments), with a further 7 mixed and 8 neutral, against 9 clearly negative. But raw positivity isn't the full story: almost every positive comment is framed in the past tense — "was," "used to," "back then." People aren't praising a brand they patronize today; they're mourning one they loved.

Single best-fit emotion

Nostalgia. It's the thread running under praise ("legendary meat pie"), grief ("it was the very place 💔") and even the complaints (franchising "destroyed" what people used to love). No other emotion touches this many comments.

Are customers happy?

Not with the brand's current state — several openly ask if it's shut down or been sold. But they remain emotionally loyal to what it was, which is a rare, reusable asset few relaunches get for free.

Where sentiment sours

Every negative comment ties back to one cause: inconsistent quality after franchising — not price, not taste memory, not competitors.

One actionable recommendation

Relaunch around the original meat pie recipe with a centralized, audited supply chain (standardized meat filling, pastry dough, and frying oil sourced from one certified kitchen per region) rather than loosely licensed franchises — then market the comeback explicitly as "the original recipe, finally consistent again," directly answering the quality complaint while cashing in on the nostalgia that's already there for free.
